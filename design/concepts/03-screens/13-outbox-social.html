Screen 13Outbox · Social · depth

The social queue · one post, many platforms, one preview that doesn't lie

Companion to screen 11 (newsletter) and screen 12 (email). The depth specific to social is per-platform fan-out: a single approved post variant is what enters the queue, but it sends as N rows — one per channel — each with its own length cap, hashtag norm, and preview shape. The detail pane is split editor on the left, live previews on the right so a reviewer can see exactly what LinkedIn, X, and Bluesky will render before clicking approve. Char counts and link-card unfurls are computed live; if a post is over X's cap and we have no thread plan, that's a hard-stop chip.

AOutbox · Social active · 3 inner views

Channels live in the sidebar. Inside Social, three inner tabs: Approval queue (drafts + pending review), Schedule (approved sends with platform pills), Sent (history + engagement). Sent currently defers to the unified Sent view; lands here when single-channel slicing earns its keep.

CFan-out · one record, N rows

In the database, one approved post becomes N rows in social_queue — one per platform — sharing a group_id. That's why the master row shows three platform pills but you reject/approve once. Per-platform overrides (alt-body, alt-image) live on the child row; if the child has no override it falls back to the parent. This is also how the "blocked" state works: rejecting one platform leaves the others scheduled.

GHow a draft gets here · the social pipeline

Blog announcement

New blog hits → cmo-agent generates 2-3 platform variants → inserted as one group with N child rows.

→

AI generation

Scheduled cmo-agent runs (Mon/Wed/Fri) draft from a topic queue. Reviewer culls.

→

Manual

Composer · ⌘⇧N. Pick platforms first, write once, alt-body any platform that needs different.

→

Reply / quote

Inbound mentions trigger a reply draft with thread context (Bluesky firehose first, X tracking limited).

→

You decide

Approve & schedule (per-platform), reject one or all, or send to chat to fix.
