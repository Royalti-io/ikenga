Sequences · the cadence is per-recipient, the calendar would lie

Sequences are special-case: a 4-step sequence enrolled with 50 recipients fires 200 actual sends, each on its own clock from when the recipient was added. So Sequences drops the Schedule tab; the inner views are Queue (drafts + pending review), Active (running enrolments — what's firing now and to whom), Sent (cohort metrics — reply rate, completion, step-level dropoff). The detail pane is a step rail: a vertical list of step cards with the delay shown between them, so you can see the cadence without doing math.

AOutbox · Sequences active · 3 inner views

Channels live in the sidebar. Inside Sequences, three inner tabs replace the standard Queue/Schedule/Sent. Schedule is gone (per-recipient cadence); Active takes its place and shows what's running. Sent is cohort-level — reply rate, completion, dropoff — rather than per-send like email.

BApproval queue · the step rail

Master lists sequences awaiting approval; detail is a vertical step rail. Each step is its own card (subject, body preview, delivery system) and the gap between cards is labelled with the delay (e.g. +3 days). One Approve & activate button at the bottom enrols the segment and starts step 1 firing on its schedule. Until then, no sends go out.

CActive · who is on which step right now

For each running sequence we render a recipient grid — one tile per enrolled recipient, coloured by current step. The grid surfaces patterns at a glance: everyone stuck on step 2 probably means step 2's subject is bad; three bounces in a row probably means a list-hygiene problem. Click a tile to open that recipient's per-step history (who got what, when, what they did).

EPause · resume · skip-step controls

Three sequence-wide controls, all reversible: Pause halts further sends across the cohort but keeps state (resumes from where each recipient was). Skip step drops the next step for everyone (e.g. step 2 was bad, everyone jumps to step 3 on its scheduled date). Stop & unenrol is the kill — recipients don't get further sends. All three log to agent_runs.

action · pause
Pauses next send for every enrolled recipient. Already-sent steps stand. Resume anytime — re-runs the cadence from each recipient's last completed step.
action · skip step
Pick a step number; everyone scheduled for that step jumps to the next one on the same date. Useful when one step is broken and the cadence is otherwise fine.
action · stop
Unenrols everyone. Sends already in flight at the delivery system go through; nothing further is scheduled. Use when the segment was wrong, not when the content was wrong.
FSent · cohort funnel · where people drop

Once a sequence completes (or you stop it), it lands in Sent as a cohort. The metric that matters is where people drop: a step with 80% open and 4% reply is fine; a step with 30% open is broken. Funnel is the only chart on this view — we resist the urge to add a timeline because cadence is already per-recipient.

GHow a sequence gets here · the pipeline

Authored

Manual (you wrote it) or vp-sales-agent (drafted from a target list). Steps are written one at a time.

→

Segment attached

Pick a segment (CRM filter or static list). Recipient count must be > 0 to approve.

→

Approved · activate

Status flips to active. Step 1 schedules immediately for every recipient on their local 9am.

→

Per-recipient cadence

Cron sweeps every 15min: for each enrolled recipient, fire next step if its delay has elapsed.

→

Completes or stops

Last step delivered → cohort → Sent. Or you stop early; partial cohort still rolls up.

IHand-off · "fix in chat" with step scope

Send-to-chat from a step card opens a Claude session seeded with just that step plus the surrounding cadence. Mirrors newsletter §J. Don't push the whole sequence into the seed — most edits are local to one step's subject or body.
