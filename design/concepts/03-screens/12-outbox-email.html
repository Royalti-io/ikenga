Screen 12Outbox · Email · depth

The email queue · with the receipt context that makes a reply easy

Companion to screen 11 (newsletter). Same shell — channels in the sidebar, views in the content. Email is the noisiest channel: replies, manual sends, sequence-spawned drafts, and automated agents (Ruby, VP Sales) all land in the same queue. The depth specific to email is reply intelligence: who is this person, what was the last touch, where do they sit in our CRM, and which sequence (if any) put this draft here. That context lives in a panel directly under the subject so a reviewer can decide in seconds — not by clicking out to Twenty CRM and back.

AOutbox · Email active · 3 inner views

Channels live in the sidebar (left of the Tauri shell). Inside Email, three inner tabs cover the lifecycle: Approval queue (drafts + pending review), Schedule (approved sends waiting on their slot), Sent (history + deliverability). Sent currently defers to the unified Sent view; lands here when the volume justifies it.

GHow a draft gets here · the email pipeline

Four sources land in the same queue. The reviewer doesn't need to care — that's why source is a chip, not a section. But knowing the shape helps when something's missing.

Inbound reply

Mbox sidecar polls Thunderbird → Ruby drafts a response → lands in queue with full thread context.

→

Manual compose

You hit ⌘⇧N from anywhere. Composer writes to email_drafts, status draft.

→

Sequence step

An active sequence fires step N for each enrolled recipient. One row per recipient.

→

Agent

Ruby (support), VP Sales, CFO each have routes that produce drafts. Marked agent.

→

You decide

Approve & schedule, send to chat to fix, or reject with a reason.
